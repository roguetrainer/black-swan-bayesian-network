# Black Swan Comparison: Eurozone Breakup vs Trump Tariffs 2025

## Two Black Swan Scenarios, Two Different Risk Profiles

This document compares two implementations of the Rebonato-Denev Bayesian network methodology for modeling "black swan" events in financial markets.

---

## Side-by-Side Comparison

| Aspect | **Eurozone Breakup** | **Trump Tariffs 2025** |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Status** | Hypothetical scenario | Explicitly stated policy |
| **Base Probability** | ~7% (normal times) | ~70% (given statements) |
| **Black Swan Probability** | ~76% (political+economic crisis) | ~90% (aggressive+retaliation) |
| **Timeline** | Years of warning signs | Immediate (Jan 2025+) |
| **Reversibility** | Extremely difficult | Politically entrenched |
| **Market Awareness** | Well understood | UNDERPRICED |
| **Historical Precedent** | None (modern era) | 2018-2019 (but scaled up) |
| **Primary Impact** | Financial system | Real economy + markets |
| **Geographic Scope** | Europe-centric | Global |
| **Policy Response** | ECB limited | Fed conflicted |

---

## Network Complexity

### Eurozone Breakup Network
- **8 variables**: Political, Economic, Breakup, Spreads, Flight, Corp Bonds, Gov Bonds, Equities
- **Focus**: Financial transmission mechanism
- **Timeframe**: Long development period
- **Key Risk**: Systemic financial crisis

### Trump Tariffs 2025 Network
- **13 variables**: Policy, Response, Escalation, Supply Chain, Inflation, Dollar, Fed, Yields, 5 Sectors
- **Focus**: Real economy transmission + multiple sectors
- **Timeframe**: Rapid onset
- **Key Risk**: Stagflationary shock

**Insight**: The tariffs network is MORE COMPLEX because it captures both real economy and financial market channels, plus multiple sectoral impacts.

## Key Differences in Market Dynamics

### Eurozone: Financial Crisis Pattern
1. Political/economic stress
2. Breakup risk rises
3. Credit markets freeze
4. Flight to quality (USD assets)
5. **Clear winners**: US Treasuries, gold, dollar

### Tariffs 2025: Stagflation Pattern
1. Policy shock (tariffs)
2. Supply disruption + inflation
3. Fed trapped (inflation vs growth)
4. Rising yields hurt all assets
5. **No clear winners**: All sectors vulnerable

**Critical Insight**: The tariffs scenario is HARDER to hedge because there's no obvious flight-to-quality trade within equities.

---

## Why Tariffs Are More Dangerous for 2025

### 1. **Higher Probability**
- Eurozone: 7% base → requires multiple failures
- Tariffs: 70% base → explicitly stated policy

### 2. **Less Predictable**
- Eurozone: Clear warning signs (spreads, CDS)
- Tariffs: Could implement overnight via executive order

### 3. **Fewer Hedges**
- Eurozone: Buy USTs, sell European assets
- Tariffs: Limited domestic safe havens

### 4. **Fed Constrained**
- Eurozone: Fed can cut rates (supporting US assets)
- Tariffs: Fed may need to raise rates (hurting all assets)

### 5. **Market Complacency**
- Eurozone: Well-understood, priced conservatively
- Tariffs: **SIGNIFICANTLY UNDERPRICED** by markets

## Causality vs Correlation Advantage

Both models demonstrate the Rebonato-Denev advantage over traditional correlation-based approaches:

### Traditional Approach Would Say:
- **Eurozone**: "No historical precedent, can't model it"
- **Tariffs**: "2018-2019 was manageable, so this will be too"

### Causal Bayesian Approach Says:
- **Eurozone**: "We understand the causal mechanism (political → financial), can model without precedent"
- **Tariffs**: "Conditions are WORSE than 2018-2019 (inflation, supply chains, Fed constraints), causally different"

**This is the power of causal modeling**: Understanding WHY things happen, not just WHAT happened before.

## Combined Scenario: What if BOTH Happen?

While unlikely, it's worth considering the interaction:

### Compound Effects
1. **Tariffs trigger global slowdown** → Strains EU economy further
2. **EU instability** → Strengthens USD more → Amplifies tariff impacts
3. **Fed conflicted** → Can't support both domestic economy and global stability
4. **Risk-off** → Multiple crises feed each other

### Probability
- Independent: 7% × 70% = 4.9%
- But likely **positively correlated** (global stress) → ~10-15%

### Impact
- Would qualify as **SYSTEMIC CRISIS** level event
- Similar to 2008 in severity (though different mechanism)
- Would require extraordinary policy response

**Takeaway**: While low probability, the tail risk of combined scenarios is non-trivial and would be catastrophic.

Both scenarios demonstrate the power of the Rebonato-Denev causal Bayesian network approach for modeling black swan events. However, they represent fundamentally different risk profiles:

### Eurozone Breakup

- Low current probability
- Well-understood by markets
- Gradual development

### Trump Tariffs 2025
- **Active, imminent threat**
- High probability (70%+)
- Significantly underpriced
- Rapid potential onset

**For 2025 specifically, the Trump tariffs represent the most significant, actionable macro risk that investors should be actively managing.**

1. Allowing us to model both scenarios despite different precedents
2. Quantifying probabilities where traditional models fail
3. Identifying transmission mechanisms for portfolio positioning
4. Providing framework for dynamic updating as events unfold

**This is exactly what causal modeling was designed for: navigating uncertainty about unprecedented events.**
