# Trump Tariffs 2025: Black Swan Bayesian Network Analysis

## Executive Summary

This Bayesian network models the **most significant macro risk for 2025**: aggressive tariff implementation under Trump's second term and potential Chinese retaliation. Unlike the Eurozone scenario, this is **not hypothetical** - Trump has explicitly stated intentions for 60%+ tariffs on China.

### Key Finding

**Markets are significantly underpricing this risk.**

- Market pricing: ~30-40% probability of aggressive implementation
- Our model base case: **70% probability** (given stated policy intentions)
- Black swan scenario probability: **~45%** (severe escalation with major economic disruption)

### 2. **Historical Precedent Shows Escalation**
- 2018-2019: Trade war reached 25% tariffs before de-escalation

### 3. **Elevated Baseline Vulnerabilities**
- **Supply chains**: More concentrated post-COVID (less redundancy)
- **Inflation**: Still above target (Fed less able to accommodate)
- **Geopolitical**: US-China relations worse than 2018
- **Political**: More domestic polarization, less compromise

### 4. **Market Complacency**
Current market pricing suggests this is viewed as:
- Negotiating tactic (it may not be)
- Gradual implementation (statements suggest otherwise)
- Manageable like 2018-2019 (conditions are worse)

- **Immediate implementation** likely (Jan 2025+)
- **Weaker economy** with less buffer
- **Elevated inflation** constrains Fed
- **Less business influence** in polarized environment
- **Political commitment** makes reversal harder

## Model Limitations and Caveats

### Uncertainties
1. **Political**: Trump may moderate positions (though less likely than markets think)
2. **China Response**: Degree of retaliation uncertain
3. **Fed Reaction Function**: Inflation vs growth prioritization unclear
4. **Business Pressure**: Corporate lobbying may succeed (though less likely)
5. **Economic Resilience**: Actual supply chain flexibility unknown

### Model Assumptions
- Binary states (reality is continuous)
- Static probabilities (dynamic adjustments needed)
- Independent sectors (correlations may be higher)
- No second-order effects (financial crises, etc.)

### What Could Make This WORSE
- China retaliates on US Treasuries (nuclear option)
- Supply chain collapse triggers financial system stress
- Multiple countries retaliate simultaneously
- Domestic political crisis compounds economic stress

### What Could Make This BETTER
- Quick de-escalation/deal (low probability)
- Supply chains more resilient than expected
- China prioritizes stability over retaliation
- Fed has more flexibility than anticipated
